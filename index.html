Future Access for All

Helping individuals and organizations care for their valuable information.

Top

Data. Digital assets. Born-digital materials.

How do you determine what is valuable? What should you keep? How do you go about caring for what you value? If you are an artist, work at a cultural institution, or direct a nonprofit organization it may feel particularly challenging to start thinking about and taking action to care for valuable information assets.

Regardless of size or resources, every organization should have the opportunity to be proactive and intentional in ensuring future access to the information they value. But planning for the future is different from focusing on the needs of the current moment.

Our approach centers on guiding and empowering the people and organizations we work with to integrate the practice of caring for information assets into the creation process itself. We collaborate with clients to develop a custom strategy that makes sense for their context, provide guidance to help them put that strategy into action, and offer continued support in maintaining and evolving this strategy over time.

Services

Consulting

We provide consulting services to individuals and organizations seeking to take an intentional approach to caring for and preparing their information assets for future access. I’ve designed my approach to establish trust, understand context, and closely collaborate to make a plan that can be put into action.

Training

We also design, develop, and facilitate custom learning and training experiences to educate and advance the knowledge and practice of individuals and organizations on topics such as digital archives, digital preservation, and information management.

Coaching

Through our coaching services, we provide a one-on-one version of training that may include personalized guidance, professional mentorship, and targeted training activities to individuals who are seeking support in areas such as developing digital preservation programs, establishing information management practices, and designing digital archives workflows.

Process

Explore

First we have a conversation where we discuss your goals, needs, current context, and priorities. I share more details about my services and process, and we start shaping and defining project details including scope, timeline, and budget.

Understand

Context is key, so the initial focus is on gathering information to gain a deep and meaningful understanding of your current situation. We have conversations to learn more about your structure, operations, workflows, infrastructure, resources, and future directions. My intention is to understand how information assets are currently created, managed, used, and valued.

Strategize

Grounded in understanding of your current context, we then work together to develop a custom strategy to ensure information assets are cared for and prepared for future access. This is a highly collaborative process where we define what digital materials are valuable and why they should be cared for; the changes and investments needed to ensure future access; and a timeline for making changes. The resulting strategy provides a foundation for growth and a customized and detailed roadmap and set of recommendations for integrating future access into your current production process.

Action + Support

With a strategy in place you are now ready to take action, to start making caring for information assets a core part of what you do. I stick with you to provide guidance and support in navigating these changes, including activities such as strategic visioning; knowledge sharing and training; process development and improvement; and system/tool selection and implementation. These shifts can take time, and I’ll be there as long as you need me.

About Sam Meister

Sam an archivist and educator based in Columbus, Ohio who has been working in and with libraries, archives, museums, galleries, artists, and nonprofit organizations for over 15 years. He is drawn to this work because he sees the potential for people to create their own archives for their creative practices, their organizations, and their communities. With Future Access For All he is seeking to expand archival thinking and practice to new contexts, to guide and support people in taking action to ensure the digital materials they are creating continue to exist for as long as they need them. Previously, Sam served as the Preservation Communities Manager at the Educopia Institute, and before that as the Digital Archivist at the University of Montana. He received my M.L.I.S from San Jose State University, and B.A. in Visual Arts from the University of California, San Diego.
